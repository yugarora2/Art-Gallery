Tanjore Painting

Romantic Overview

Kolam art feels like beauty created and erased every day. Early in the morning, before the world wakes up, intricate patterns appear at the doorstep—only to fade away by evening. The romance here is gentle and philosophical. It teaches impermanence—that beauty doesn’t need to last forever to matter. There’s also warmth in it; these designs welcome guests, invite positivity, and reflect a quiet love for home, routine, and tradition.

History

Kolam has been practiced in Tamil Nadu for centuries as part of daily life. • Drawn at the entrance of homes every morning • Especially important during festivals like Pongal • Traditionally made using rice flour, which also feeds ants and birds It reflects a cultural idea that art should be part of everyday living, not just special occasions.

Art Techniques

Kolam is known for its precision and symmetry: • Dot Grid System Patterns are created by connecting or looping around dots • Geometric & Symmetrical Designs Highly balanced and mathematically structured • White Powder (Rice Flour) Traditionally used, though colors are added during festivals • Freehand Skill Drawn without tools—requires practice and control • • Continuous Line Patterns Many designs are drawn in one unbroken line

Artist

Kolam is traditionally created by women in households. • Passed from mother to daughter • Practiced daily as part of routine • Not considered a profession, but a cultural habit and expression It’s one of the few art forms that is both universal and deeply personal.
